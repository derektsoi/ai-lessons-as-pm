# AI Lessons as a PM

What I've learned about AI — from the perspective of a product manager who builds with it, not just talks about it.

## What This Is

A collection of real lessons, mistakes, and mental model shifts from using AI tools in product work. Not theory — things I've actually experienced and changed my mind about.

## Why This Exists

Writing these lessons is how I process what I've learned. The act of articulating a lesson — finding the right framing, questioning my own assumptions, separating what actually mattered from what felt important in the moment — is the learning. Publishing is a side effect.

## Topics

1. [Building product demos with Claude Code](lessons/01-building-demos-with-claude-code.md) — Using AI to generate directional mockups for quarterly planning, instead of waiting for a design sprint.
2. [Helping designers prototype with Claude Code](lessons/02-designers-prototyping-with-claude-code.md) — Early experiments in enabling non-engineers to use Claude Code for UI prototyping.
3. _The old safety net is gone and we don't have a replacement yet. Slow iteration used to catch weak judgment — designer pushback in week 2, engineer flags in week 4. Now AI-built demos look authoritative before anyone checks the thinking._ (future)

## About

I'm a PM based in Hong Kong. I started using AI tools seriously in my work and personal projects, and found that most "AI for PMs" content is either too hype-driven or too abstract. This is my attempt to share what actually matters.
